## Risk Assessment

The 1896 catalog accounts for 27 woodcuts in the Polyandrion. We have all 27 mapped to 14 pages. The question is whether any of these 27 are at wrong pages (off by 1), or whether there are additional small woodcuts (decorative initials, small ornaments) not in the catalog.

**Likelihood of missing catalog woodcuts:** LOW. The 1896 catalog is comprehensive. If anything, our mapping might have wrong page assignments for the INFERRED entries (248, 252, 258, 260).

**Likelihood of uncatalogued woodcuts:** LOW-MEDIUM. The Polyandrion does not have the decorative initials that appear in other sections. But some pages might have small inscription cartouches or ornamental borders that could count as minor woodcuts.

## For Scholars in the Meat World

To fully verify this section, a researcher with access to the physical book (or a high-quality digital facsimile at page level) should:

1. **Check pages 234, 239, 241, 243, 245, 247, 249, 251** for any small woodcuts not in the 1896 catalog
2. **Verify pages 248, 252, 258, 260** -- these are INFERRED assignments; confirm the correct woodcuts are on these pages
3. **Photograph or scan any marginalia** in the Polyandrion section -- this is the thematic heart of the memento mori tradition and Russell's annotator may have responded strongly to the death imagery
4. **Compare epitaph inscriptions** against Pozzi & Ciapponi's transcriptions for any variants in the Seville copy vs the Venice copy

This verification work cannot be done remotely. It requires eyes on the physical artifact or a complete page-level digitization we can navigate freely.
