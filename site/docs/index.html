Project Documents

This section makes the project legible as a research build rather than a black box. It gathers 18 internal methodological and reflective documents—ontology notes, concordance method, boundary audits, proposals, mistakes, aesthetic reviews, and related planning texts—that explain what the project thinks it is doing and where its assumptions have shifted over time.

These documents are part of the scholarly apparatus, not just engineering residue. They record how the database, site, and interpretive claims were assembled, where the strongest arguments lie, and where the project has had to revise itself.

About This Project

A digital humanities project documenting the readership and marginalia of the 1499 Aldine Hypnerotomachia Poliphili, based on James Russell's PhD thesis (Durham, 2014).
