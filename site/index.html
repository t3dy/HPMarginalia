Marginalia of the Hypnerotomachia Poliphili

Annotations from Two Copies of the 1499 Aldine Edition

Based on the doctoral research of James Russell

The Hypnerotomachia Poliphili, published by Aldus Manutius in Venice in 1499, is one of the most celebrated and enigmatic books of the Italian Renaissance. Its dense prose, interweaving classical languages with architectural description and allegorical narrative, invited generations of readers to leave their marks in the margins.

This gallery presents marginal annotations from two surviving copies studied by James Russell in his PhD dissertation (Durham, 2014): the British Library copy (shelfmark C.60.o.12, bearing annotations by Ben Jonson and an anonymous alchemist) and the Biblioteca degli Intronati copy in Siena (shelfmark O.III.38). Each entry pairs the manuscript image with Russell's analysis of the annotation found there.

Beyond the gallery, this site offers a folio-by-folio index of annotated pages, profiles of 30 scholars whose work spans five centuries of HP studies, a bibliography of 109 editions and scholarly works, and a dictionary of 37 essential terms for reading the book and its scholarship. The project's methodology documents and pipeline scripts are published for transparency.
