Woodcut: Palace interior with seven planetary compartments

Woodcut of an ornate interior architectural facade with seven compartments/niches, each labeled with planetary names. Visible labels: MERCVRIVS, VENVS, SOL, MARS, and partially visible others (LVNA, IVPITER, SATVRNVS). Rich ornamental borders with floral/arabesque patterns. This is the palace of the seven planets — a key cosmological/astrological scene in the HP.

Scholarly Significance

The planetary palace woodcut is architecturally and cosmologically significant. The reader's annotations connecting planetary compartments to precious stones (Smaragdo/emerald, Saphico/sapphire) reveal an astrological-lapidary reading of the HP's architecture. If the word 'Sulphure' is correctly read, this page becomes another alchemical annotation site: a reader is explicitly connecting the planetary symbolism to the sulphur-mercury theory of metals. This would be consistent with Hand B's program elsewhere in the volume.
