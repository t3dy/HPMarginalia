Book History & Bibliography
A book printed before 1501, during the infancy of European printing.
The 1499 HP is an incunabulum (plural: incunabula), produced in the last year of the fifteenth century. It is catalogued in the Incunabula Short Title Catalogue (ISTC) maintained by the British Library. Russell's world census was conducted primarily through ISTC records. The HP is widely regarded as the most elaborately illustrated incunabulum ever produced.

Why It Matters for the Hypnerotomachia

The 1499 HP is widely regarded as the most elaborately illustrated incunabulum ever produced. Its 172 woodcuts, historiated initials, and pseudo-hieroglyphic inscriptions represent the highest point of Aldine typographic ambition before Aldus shifted to smaller-format Greek texts.

Why It Matters in Scholarship

Russell's world census of annotated copies was conducted primarily through Incunabula Short Title Catalogue (ISTC) records. The HP's status as an incunabulum places it within the well-catalogued universe of fifteenth-century printed books, enabling systematic identification of surviving copies.
