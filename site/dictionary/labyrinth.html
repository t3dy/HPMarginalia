Labyrinth Draft

Architecture & Built Form
Maze structures in the HP that Poliphilo navigates, combining garden design with allegorical significance.
The HP describes labyrinthine structures that function both as garden features and as allegories of the questing mind. Poliphilo's navigation of these mazes parallels his broader journey from confusion to understanding. The labyrinth connects the HP to classical myth (Theseus and the Minotaur) and to Renaissance garden design, where hedge mazes served as sites of aristocratic recreation and philosophical contemplation.

Why It Matters in Scholarship

The labyrinth connects the HP to classical myth (Theseus and the Minotaur) and to Renaissance garden design. Fabiani Giannetto (2015) reads the HP's labyrinths within the wider context of meraviglia — architectural structures designed to produce wonder.
