Signature Unreviewed

Book History & Bibliography
A letter-number code printed at the foot of certain pages to guide the binder in assembling the book.
In early printed books, signatures are marks (usually a lowercase letter followed by a numeral) placed at the bottom of the first recto of each leaf in a gathering. The 1499 HP uses signatures a-z (omitting j, u, w) then A-G, each gathering consisting of eight leaves. Russell's thesis references folios by signature (e.g., b6v) rather than by page number, since no page numbers were printed in the original.
Sources: Standard bibliographic terminology; Russell 2014 passim
