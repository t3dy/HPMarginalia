Signature Draft

Book History & Bibliography
A letter-number code printed at the foot of certain pages to guide the binder in assembling the book.
In early printed books, signatures are marks (usually a lowercase letter followed by a numeral) placed at the bottom of the first recto of each leaf in a gathering. The 1499 HP uses signatures a-z (omitting j, u, w) then A-G, each gathering consisting of eight leaves. Russell's thesis references folios by signature (e.g., b6v) rather than by page number, since no page numbers were printed in the original.

Why It Matters for the Hypnerotomachia

The HP uses signatures a-z (omitting j, u, w) then A-G, each gathering consisting of eight leaves. Russell references all folios by signature rather than page number, making the signature system the primary navigational framework for both the original book and this project's concordance pipeline.

Why It Matters in Scholarship

Russell (2014) adopts signature-based referencing throughout his thesis because the 1499 HP has no printed pagination. This practice, standard in incunabular bibliography, allows scholars to locate passages precisely across different copies regardless of later pagination schemes.
