Hieroglyph Unreviewed

Textual & Visual Motifs
A picture-writing system; in the HP, pseudo-Egyptian symbolic inscriptions that Poliphilo encounters and interprets.
The HP contains numerous pseudo-hieroglyphic inscriptions that Poliphilo deciphers during his journey. These are not authentic Egyptian hieroglyphs but Renaissance inventions inspired by the rediscovery of Horapollo's Hieroglyphica. Curran (1998) situates them within fifteenth-century humanist Egyptology. Priki analyzes their narrative function, arguing they serve as structural devices within the dream journey rather than mere antiquarian decoration. The hieroglyphs influenced the later emblem tradition.
Sources: Curran 1998; Priki (undated); Gombrich 1972
