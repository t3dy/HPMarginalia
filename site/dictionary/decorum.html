Decorum Draft

Aesthetic Concepts
The classical principle of fitness or appropriateness — matching style to subject, ornament to function — applied throughout the HP.
The HP applies the classical principle of decorum to its architectural and garden descriptions: each structure is ornamented according to its purpose, and each garden element is placed according to its symbolic function. Poliphilo notes where decorum is observed and where it is violated. This attention to fitness connects the HP to the Vitruvian tradition and to Alberti's insistence that beauty arises from the rational correspondence between part and whole.
