Scholarly Debates
The HP as a work of Italian vernacular literature, adapting Petrarchan conventions into a prose-image hybrid.
Trippe (2002) argues that the HP has been understudied as literature, with scholars treating it primarily as architectural or philosophical. Through close analysis of woodcuts and their accompanying text, she demonstrates how the author adapted Petrarchan tropes -- the beloved's beauty, the lover's suffering -- into an interplay of word and image. The HP's invented language operates within recognizable Italian vernacular poetic frameworks rather than being merely eccentric.
Sources: Trippe 2002
