Dream Narrative Draft

Scholarly Debates
A literary work framed as a dream, a genre to which the HP belongs alongside the Roman de la Rose.
The HP is structured as a dream within a dream: Poliphilo falls asleep and enters a vision, within which he falls asleep again and enters a deeper dream (the dream-within-a-dream beginning at a6v). Priki (2016) places the HP in a comparative tradition with the Roman de la Rose and the Byzantine Tale of Livistros and Rodamne, arguing that the dream frame enables pedagogical encounters that prepare the lover for union with the beloved. Gollnick (1999) provides a theoretical framework for dream-narrative hermeneutics through his study of Apuleius.
