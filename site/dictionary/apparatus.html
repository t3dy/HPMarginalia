Book History & Bibliography
The scholarly infrastructure surrounding a critical edition: introduction, notes, bibliography, indexes.
The HP has been the subject of two major critical editions: Pozzi & Ciapponi (1964) and Ariani & Gabriele (1998, Adelphi). Godwin's 1999 English translation (Thames & Hudson) provides the most accessible modern apparatus. The term 'apparatus criticus' refers specifically to the textual notes recording variant readings across copies and editions.

Why It Matters for the Hypnerotomachia

The HP lacks a printed commentary or apparatus in either of its Aldine editions (1499, 1545). The commentary tradition is instead supplied by readers' marginalia, which Russell argues collectively constitute an informal apparatus built by successive generations of annotators.

Why It Matters in Scholarship

The absence of a printed apparatus distinguishes the HP from most classical texts of the period. Pozzi and Ciapponi's 1964 critical edition provides the first modern scholarly apparatus, including textual notes and a glossary of the HP's unusual vocabulary.
