Ruined Temple Draft

Places & Settings
The great ruined classical temple Poliphilo explores early in his journey, establishing the HP's antiquarian and architectural program.
Poliphilo encounters a vast ruined temple shortly after escaping the dark forest. He describes its columns, capitals, entablatures, and inscriptions in obsessive architectural detail, establishing the ekphrastic mode that dominates the rest of the narrative. The temple's ruins embody the Renaissance topos of antiquity's grandeur and loss. Griggs (1998) reads this passage as the HP's closest approach to the antiquarian tradition of Cyriacus of Ancona, where encountering ruins generates knowledge through careful observation.
