Activity Book Unreviewed

Annotation Studies
Russell's concept of the HP as a text designed to provoke active, playful reader engagement.
Drawing on analogy with modern educational media, Russell reframes the HP as a 'humanistic activity book' in which readers cultivated ingegno through creative engagement with the text and its woodcuts. The HP's puzzles, obscure language, embedded inscriptions, and visual-textual interplay invited readers to decode, annotate, cross-reference, and extend the text in their own marginalia. This concept shifts attention from the author's intent to the reader's creative practice.
Sources: Russell 2014, pp. 34-42
