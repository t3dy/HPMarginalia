# The Hypnerotomachia Poliphili and the Woodcut as Mirror

**Christopher J. Nygren** | Word & Image 31:2 2015

## Summary

Nygren analyzes the HP's woodcuts as reflective surfaces that mirror and transform the textual narrative, arguing that the illustrations function not as transparent windows onto the described scenes but as interpretive mediations that reshape the reader's understanding. The article draws on art historical theories of the mirror and the image to develop a new framework for understanding how visual and verbal representation interact in the HP. Nygren's approach offers fresh theoretical tools for the study of illustrated books more broadly.
