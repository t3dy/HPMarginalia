# Hypnerotomachia Joins the Party: Reading across Word and Image

**William B. Keller** | Word & Image 31:2 2015

## Summary

Keller explores how the HP's interplay of text and woodcut illustration creates a distinctive reading experience that requires simultaneous engagement with verbal and visual modes. The article argues that modern scholarly approaches that separate textual analysis from art historical analysis fundamentally misrepresent the HP, which was designed as an integrated multimedia work. Keller calls for more interdisciplinary approaches to the HP that take both word and image seriously as co-equal carriers of meaning.
