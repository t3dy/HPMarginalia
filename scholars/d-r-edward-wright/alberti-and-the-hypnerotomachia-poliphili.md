# Alberti and the Hypnerotomachia Poliphili

**D. R. Edward Wright** | Journal of the Warburg and Courtauld Institutes 47 1984

## Summary

Wright examines the relationship between Leon Battista Alberti's architectural writings and the HP, arguing for significant Albertian influence on the text's descriptions of buildings and spatial design. The article traces specific correspondences between Alberti's De re aedificatoria and passages in the HP, suggesting that the author of the HP was deeply familiar with Alberti's theoretical framework. Wright contributes to the authorship debate by considering whether this Albertian influence supports or undermines the traditional Colonna attribution.
